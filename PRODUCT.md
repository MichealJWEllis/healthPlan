# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Micheal Ellis, 45, Toledo OH. Single user; this is his personal health command center, not a multi-user app. Full stack developer, systems thinker, detail-oriented. Context: keto OMAD diet, multi-day water fasts, 258.4 → 202.5 lb goal on a 6-month commitment window. Primary use is phone-in-hand quick check-ins (morning weigh-in, habit taps, glancing at the fasting timer); desktop is the secondary review surface for trends and charts. He responds to concrete numbers, streaks, and visible progress. The known risk the design must fight: abandoning routines at the friction point in the middle.

## Product Purpose

A self-built, Supabase-backed personal tracker (weight, body composition, daily workout/meal check-ins, Zero-style fasting timer) on GitHub Pages. It exists to make the invisible middle of a 6-month body recomposition visible and non-abandonable. Success looks like daily use without friction: log in seconds, see progress honestly, keep the streak alive.

## Brand Personality

Mission control with a corner man. Dense, stat-forward, war-room serious; but it celebrates real wins like a scoreboard and speaks one line of encouragement like a coach. Three words: focused, honest, rallying. It never nags, never sugarcoats, never gamifies.

## Anti-references

- Fitness-app candy: MyFitnessPal-style gamification. Badge showers, confetti, mascots, points for nothing, notification nagging.
- Register floor: interchangeable admin-template SaaS. The tool should disappear into the task.

## Design Principles

1. **Log in seconds.** Every daily action (weigh-in, check-in, fast start/end) reachable with a thumb within the first screen-length on a phone.
2. **Numbers are the hero.** Data earns the visual weight; decoration never outranks a stat.
3. **Celebrate real wins only.** Streaks, milestones, and fasting-stage transitions get moments. Nothing else does. Earned, not sprinkled.
4. **One encouraging voice.** A single rotating motivational line, placed deliberately. The app coaches once per view; it never nags.
5. **Honest progress.** Plateaus and gained weight render plainly. Trust comes from the tracker never flattering him.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Single known user, mid-40s eyes on a phone at arm's length: body text ≥14px-equivalent on mobile, WCAG AA contrast floor on the dark surface, respect prefers-reduced-motion, touch targets ≥44px for daily-action buttons.
