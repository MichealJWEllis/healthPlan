Sunday Meal Prep

Do this after Sunday lunch. Everything runs simultaneously. You are actively working about 15 minutes — the rest is hands-off oven and stove time.

How to run this efficiently: Start the oven first. Get lentils on the stove. While those cook, prep and load the sheet pans. Put eggs on last since they're quickest. Everything finishes within the same 30–35 minute window.
1
Bake Salmon Fillets feeds Mon + Sat dinner
Bake 2–3 fillets at 400°F · 12–15 min · olive oil, garlic, lemon, salt, pepper, red pepper flakes. Monday you reheat in a pan with olive oil. Saturday you cook one fresh for dinner.
⏱ 15 min hands-off
2
Sheet Pan Vegetables feeds Mon–Fri sides
Two sheet pans at 425°F. Pan 1: cauliflower florets + asparagus spears. Pan 2: cubed sweet potato (takes 30 min, start first). Toss everything in olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder, turmeric. Single layer only — no piling. Flip halfway.
⏱ 25–30 min hands-off
3
Roasted Bell Peppers feeds Wed + Thu
Slice 2–3 bell peppers into strips. Toss with olive oil, salt, smoked paprika. Can share the sheet pan with cauliflower — they take the same time.
⏱ 20–25 min hands-off
4
Cook Lentils feeds Wed + Fri
Rinse 1 cup dry lentils. Sauté garlic in olive oil 1 min. Add 1 tsp cumin + 1 tsp turmeric. Add lentils + 1 can diced tomatoes + 2.5 cups water. Boil then simmer covered 25–30 min. Finish with lemon + olive oil.
⏱ 30 min hands-off after setup
5
Hard Boil 6 Eggs feeds Wed + Fri + backup
Cold water start. Bring to boil. Off heat, lid on, 11 minutes. Transfer immediately to ice water. Peel when cool. Store unpeeled or peeled — both last 5 days in fridge.
⏱ 15 min hands-off
6
Salad Dressing Jar feeds all week
Into a jar: 3 tbsp olive oil · 1 tbsp red wine vinegar · 1 tsp Dijon mustard · 1 minced garlic clove · salt + pepper. Seal and shake until emulsified. Lasts all week in fridge.
⏱ 3 min active
7
Wash & Dry Greens feeds all week
Wash spinach + arugula. Dry thoroughly in a salad spinner or pat with paper towels. Store in a container lined with paper towel. Wet greens go bad fast — drying them makes them last 5–6 days.
⏱ 5 min active
What's in your fridge after prep Cooked salmon · roasted cauliflower · roasted asparagus · roasted sweet potato · roasted bell peppers · cooked lentils · 6 hard boiled eggs · washed greens · dressing jar. That's Monday through Friday handled. Every meal is a 2-minute plate job.

Cooking Instructions

Reference only. You run these on Sunday during prep — not during the week.

1 · Baked Salmon

15 min · Sunday prep batch · Monday dinner + Saturday dinner
  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line baking sheet with parchment.
  2. Rub salmon with 1 tbsp olive oil on both sides.
  3. Press 1–2 minced garlic cloves onto the top.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, red pepper flakes. Squeeze half a lemon over.
  5. Bake 12–15 min until it flakes easily with a fork.
  6. Drizzle more olive oil after it comes out.
To reheat Monday: pan on medium-low, small amount of olive oil, 3–4 min per side. Do not microwave — it dries out.

2 · Sheet Pan Vegetables

25–30 min · Sunday prep · feeds Mon–Fri sides
  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Start sweet potato first — cube it, toss in olive oil + salt + pepper, roast 30 min on its own sheet.
  3. Cut cauliflower into florets, asparagus into 2-inch pieces, bell peppers into strips.
  4. Toss each in olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder, turmeric.
  5. Spread in a single layer only — piling causes steaming, not roasting.
  6. Roast 20–25 min, flip halfway through.
Storage: separate containers per vegetable makes plating faster all week. Reheat in a pan with olive oil — never microwave.

3 · Anti-Inflammatory Lentils

30 min · Sunday prep · feeds Wed + Fri
  1. Rinse 1 cup dry lentils in cold water.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in medium pot over medium heat.
  3. Add 3 minced garlic cloves — sauté 1 min until fragrant.
  4. Add 1 tsp cumin + 1 tsp turmeric — stir 30 seconds.
  5. Add lentils + 1 can diced tomatoes + 2.5 cups water.
  6. Boil then reduce to simmer, cover, cook 25–30 min until soft.
  7. Finish with salt + squeeze of lemon + drizzle of olive oil.
Storage: 5 days in fridge. Freezes well. Reheat in a pan with a splash of water to loosen.

4 · Salad Dressing Jar

3 min · Sunday prep · lasts all week
  1. 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  2. 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  3. 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  4. 1 minced garlic clove · salt + pepper
  5. Seal jar and shake until emulsified. Done.

5 · Hard Boiled Eggs

15 min hands-off · Sunday prep · feeds Wed + Fri
  1. Place 6 eggs in a pot, cover with cold water.
  2. Bring to a full boil over medium-high heat.
  3. Turn off heat. Lid on. 11 minutes exactly.
  4. Transfer immediately to ice water. Let sit 5 min.
  5. Store in fridge up to 5 days. Peel as needed.

6 · Anti-Inflammatory Smoothie

5 min · Sunday breakfast only
  1. 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  2. Large handful frozen spinach
  3. ½ cup frozen blueberries + ½ cup frozen tart cherries
  4. 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  5. ½ tsp turmeric + pinch black pepper + pinch ground ginger
  6. Optional: 1 tbsp ground flaxseed
  7. Blend until smooth. Drink immediately.
No banana. No sweetener. No protein powder with seed oils. If needed, add 2–3 frozen mango chunks only.

7 · Black Bean Bowl

15 min · Sunday lunch
  1. Drain and rinse 1 can black beans.
  2. Heat in pan with olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, garlic, salt — 5 min.
  3. Build bowl: roasted sweet potato base (from Sunday prep) + seasoned black beans + roasted bell peppers + avocado.
  4. Dress with dressing jar. Top with pomegranate seeds if available.

8 · Scrambled Eggs (Weekend Breakfast)

10 min · Saturday breakfast · cooked fresh
  1. Whisk 3 eggs with salt + pepper.
  2. Heat pan over medium-low with 1 tsp olive oil.
  3. Pour eggs in, add handful of spinach immediately.
  4. Stir slowly and remove from heat while still slightly wet — they finish off the heat.
  5. Add turmeric + red pepper flakes after plating.
